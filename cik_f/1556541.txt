and Deutsche Bank Trust Company Americas (&ldquo;DBTCA&rdquo;) in New York State Supreme Court purportedly on behalf of and for
the benefit of 544&nbsp;private-label residential mortgage backed securities (&ldquo;RMBS&rdquo;) trusts asserting claims for alleged
violations of the U.S. Trust Indenture Act of&nbsp;1939 (TIA), breach of contract, breach of fiduciary duty and negligence based
on DBNTC&rsquo;s and DBTCA&rsquo;s alleged failure to perform their duties as trustees for the trusts. Plaintiffs subsequently
dismissed their state court complaint and filed a derivative and class action complaint in the U.S. District Court for the Southern
District of New York (the &ldquo;SDNY Action&rdquo;) on behalf of and for the benefit of 564&nbsp;private-label RMBS trusts, which
substantially overlapped with the trusts at issue in the state court action. The complaint alleges that the trusts at issue have
suffered total realized collateral losses of U.S. $89.4&nbsp;billion, but the complaint does not include a demand for money damages
in a sum certain. DBNTC and DBTCA filed a motion to dismiss, and on January 19, 2016, the court partially granted the motion on
procedural grounds:&nbsp;as to the 500 trusts that are governed by Pooling and Servicing Agreements, the court declined to exercise
jurisdiction. The court did not rule on substantive defenses asserted in the motion to dismiss as to the 64 trusts formed under
indentures for which it retained jurisdiction.&nbsp; Instead, the court ordered plaintiffs to file an amended complaint as to those
indenture trusts.&nbsp;On February 23, 2016, plaintiffs filed an amended complaint as to 62 of the 64 indenture trusts included
in the original U.S. District Court complaint.&nbsp;DBNTC and DBTCA will have an opportunity to file new defensive motions with
respect to this amended complaint. It is anticipated that plaintiffs will, in the near future, file a new state court complaint
as to some or all of the 500 trusts governed by Pooling and Servicing Agreements which were dismissed from the U.S. District Court
action.</P>
